New scarf seam settings for a less prominent Z seam

Eight new experimental settings have been added that make tuning the Z seam on your prints easier, including options for using a scarf seam. A scarf seam is created by varying the flow rate at the start and end of a line to merge the seam together, reducing the prominent bump of a traditional seam. It is named after the scarf joint used in woodworking. Visit our Thingiverse page to download these 3D printer sneakers to try it for yourself.
